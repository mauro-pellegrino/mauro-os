# Skill: YouTube Brand Breakdown
**Version:** 2.0
**Updated:** 14 July 2026 (retargeted to Mauro's channel: AI systems for agency owners)
**Input:** Brand or operator name + their top 3 highest-performing content pieces + 6 most representative long-running pieces
**Output:** Full structured breakdown ready for YouTube script and Miro board: personas, awareness level thoughts, angles, formats, what works, the system reveal

---

## Required Reading

Before producing any written output, read `brand/voice.md` and `brand/audience.md` in full. All copy must pass the 60-Second Pre-Publish Checklist at the bottom of voice.md. Ground every audience-facing decision (segment, stated pain, language) in the ICP in audience.md.

## What This Skill Does

Produces the full analytical and scripting foundation for a YouTube breakdown video on Mauro's channel. The subject is a brand, agency, or operator that wins clients through content: a personal brand pulling inbound, an agency with a working content engine, or a creator whose posts visibly convert.

The breakdown has two jobs: show the viewer (an established agency owner) something genuinely useful about how a working inbound content system operates, and demonstrate Mauro's process in a way that builds trust without a hard sell.

The output feeds directly into a YouTube script and Miro board brief.

---

## Inputs

**Step 1: Name only (user provides)**
Claude researches the subject first: what they sell, who they serve, where they publish, scale indicators, funnel structure. Output this before asking for content samples.

**Step 2: Content samples (user provides)**
- Top 3 highest-performing pieces (copy or transcript + format description)
- Top 6 most representative or longest-running pieces (copy or transcript + format description)

Do not proceed to analysis until both steps are complete.

---

## Research Phase (Step 1)

When given the name, fetch and compile:

1. **Homepage / offer page**: what they sell, core claim, positioning, who it's for
2. **Content channels**: X, LinkedIn, YouTube, newsletter. Posting cadence, follower scale, engagement patterns visible from public data
3. **Funnel structure**: lead magnets, booking links, newsletters, DMs-as-CTA, gated assets. How a stranger becomes a call.
4. **Third-party signals**: testimonials, reviews, podcast appearances, what real customers or followers say, recurring language
5. **Revenue/scale indicators**: public interviews, verified statements, press. If no verifiable number exists, say so explicitly. Never estimate or guess revenue.

Output a Subject Overview section covering:
- What they sell and who they sell it to
- Their offer and pricing structure (if public)
- Content footprint (channels, cadence, audience scale)
- Notable funnel elements (lead magnets, auto-DMs, booking flows, newsletters)
- Verified revenue or scale indicator. If unverifiable, lead with audience and output volume instead.

### Why They're Winning: Market Position Read

As part of the Subject Overview, include a short section answering: why is this operator's content actually producing clients?

This is not a summary of their marketing. It is a read on the structural reason they are winning. Two possible frames:

**Saturated lane:** if they operate in a crowded lane (marketing advice, agency growth, AI content), the question is: what is their differentiation? Is it proof density, a named system, a specific ICP, a format they own, or distribution mechanics (auto-DMs, lead magnets)? Where did the lane zig and they zagged? Name the specific decision that gives them an edge over everyone else in the feed.

**New lane:** if they're early in a lane or creating demand rather than capturing it, the question is: what is the new behaviour or belief they're selling? What had to be true in the market for this to work now that wasn't true two years ago?

In either case, the goal is one clear sentence: the structural reason this operator is winning that others haven't figured out or aren't executing as well. This is the frame the viewer needs before the content analysis lands.

If the reason is primarily offer (not content), say so. If it's primarily content and distribution, say so. If it's both, explain how the two reinforce each other.

## Analysis Phase (Step 2)

Once content samples are provided, run the following in order. Do not skip steps or reorder them.

---

### Section 1: Persona Analysis

Identify the personas being targeted across all 9 pieces combined.

For each persona ask:
- Who is this person specifically? Role, business context, relationship with the problem.
- Is this persona broad (anyone with this symptom) or specific (defined situation, identity, context)?
- How many of the 9 pieces are pointed at this persona?

Flag if personas are too broad for 2026. The standard has shifted: clear persona definition with specific context outperforms broad demographic targeting. If their personas are vague, say so and name what a more specific version would look like.

Select the 3 primary personas. All 3 must be for the core offer/funnel, not for different products in their range. If they are targeting different offers with different personas, note this, then focus analysis on the persona tied to the main offer.

Flag any personas that are absent from their content but could work well for the core offer, especially if comparable operators are publishing to those audiences.

---

### Section 2: Awareness Level Thoughts Per Persona

For each of the 3 personas, map the prospect's internal monologue at each awareness level.

These are not descriptions of what the subject is publishing at each level. These are what the prospect is thinking before they encounter the content.

Format for each persona:

**Persona name**
- Unaware: "[internal thought]"
- Problem Aware: "[internal thought]"
- Solution Aware: "[internal thought]"
- Product Aware: "[internal thought]"
- Most Aware: "[internal thought]"

Then note which awareness levels the subject's current content is actually targeting. Flag over-concentration (e.g. 70% of pieces at Most Aware) and what it means for their growth ceiling.

---

### Section 3: Winning Angles

Identify the top 3 angles being run across the 9 pieces.

For each angle:
- Name it in one line
- Which pieces demonstrate it (by number or description)
- Why it works: the psychological mechanism, not just the description
- Any executional observation (is it being run well, is the structure the same across every piece, is it getting repetitive)

Then identify missing angles: angles that would work for the core offer and persona set but are absent from their content. Ground this in the awareness level map: if Problem Aware is underserved, what angle would reach someone at that level?

---

### Section 4: Formats

Break down the format mix across the 9 pieces:
- List each format present (text post, thread, long-form article, doc carousel, talking-head video, faceless video, lead-magnet post, case study, build-in-public log, etc.)
- Approximate % of their output each format represents based on what's visible
- For each format: one observation about why it's working or not working

**Missing formats: two sources.**

First, flag any format absent from this subject's mix that would suit the persona and awareness level gaps identified in Sections 1-3.

Second, identify what comparable fast-growing operators in the same lane are currently running that this subject isn't. This is the competitive format gap: not hypothetical, observed. If operators at the same scale or higher are running a format that's absent here, name it, say which type of operator is running it, and explain why it's gaining traction. This gives the viewer something they can act on immediately.

**Format Concentration Check**

After mapping the formats, assess repetition risk.

If 70%+ of the subject's output is one format or one structural template (same opener style, same post shape, same CTA), the audience starts scrolling past it and reach compresses. Ask: if you stripped the topics and looked only at structure, how many of these 9 pieces are effectively the same piece?

Flag the risk level:
- **Low risk:** clear format diversity across the output, different structures reaching different audience pockets
- **Medium risk:** 1-2 dominant formats with minimal structural variation, engagement flattening
- **High risk:** the majority of output is structurally identical, engagement declining, new posts cannibalising attention from old ones

Note this explicitly in the section. It sets up the System Reveal.

---

### Section 5: What Works (Viewer Takeaway)

This section is written for the YouTube viewer. It is the most shareable, most educational part of the video.

Identify the 1-2 things this subject is doing at a system level that actually works: not just "they post consistently" but the specific structural or strategic decision that explains their results.

For each, write:
- The observation (one clear sentence)
- The mechanism (why this works, at the psychological or distribution level)
- The takeaway (what an agency owner could apply to their own brand)

This section should make the viewer feel they learned something they couldn't have figured out just by scrolling the subject's feed. That is the standard.

---

### Section 6: The System Reveal

This is the closing section of the video. It is not a hard sell. It is a demonstration of how Mauro's engine would approach the same problem.

Structure:

**What they currently have: the system snapshot**
Before saying what to change, map what this subject actually has: which personas they're targeting, which awareness levels they're hitting, which angles they're running, and which formats they're using. One short paragraph is enough. The analysis in Sections 1-5 has already done the work; this is the synthesis.

**What to preserve**
Pick 1-2 things from their system worth keeping. Explain why. This shows the viewer that the approach is analytical, not reflexively "I'd do it differently."

**What to change**
Pick 2-3 specific changes Mauro's process would make. These must connect directly back to the gaps identified in Sections 1-4: underserved personas, missing awareness levels, absent angles, format gaps, or format concentration risk.

Write each change as a specific brief, not a general recommendation:
- Who the piece would target (persona + awareness level)
- What the angle would be
- What format it would use
- How AI carries the production load (which part of the pipeline drafts it, what the human pass checks)
- Why this would outperform what they're currently running

Where format concentration risk is medium or high, frame at least one recommended change explicitly as a structurally different format: different shape, different opener style, different container, so it reaches an audience pocket the current output never touches.

**What's working for comparable operators right now**
Before closing, name 1-2 moves that are working for comparable operators in the same lane who are currently growing fast. These are not hypotheticals: they are observed patterns. Explain why these moves are gaining traction now. If Mauro is running something similar in his own engine, reference the approach and what it produced ([confirmed numbers only, otherwise bracket placeholders]).

This positions the recommendations as grounded in live market intelligence, not just gap analysis.

**The process point**
One paragraph explaining how Mauro approaches this for his own engine: source real work, map personas to awareness levels to angles, let AI draft, keep a human pass, measure against booked calls. Written in Mauro's voice per `brand/voice.md`. Not a pitch. A window into how the work gets done.

This section should end with the viewer thinking: "This is a real process, and I want it running on my agency." That is the only goal. Do not write a CTA inside the skill; the video CTA is handled separately by `skills/youtube/youtube-hook-script.md`.

---

## Output Order

Produce in this exact order:

1. Subject Overview (from research), including Why They're Winning
2. Persona Analysis
3. Awareness Level Thoughts Per Persona
4. Winning Angles
5. Formats, including competitor format benchmarks
6. What Works
7. The System Reveal: system snapshot, what to preserve, what to change, what comparable operators are doing

---

## Rules

- Never guess revenue. If no verifiable public figure exists, lead with audience scale and output volume as the indicator.
- All 3 personas must be for the same core offer/funnel.
- Awareness level thoughts are the prospect's internal monologue, not a description of what the subject is publishing.
- The "What Works" section must teach something that isn't obvious from scrolling the subject's feed.
- The System Reveal must connect directly to the gaps found in the analysis. Never introduce new observations in Section 6 that weren't grounded in Sections 1-4.
- Never write a hard sell in Section 6. The demonstration is the pitch.
- Never fabricate a number for Mauro's own engine. Confirmed numbers or bracket placeholders only.
- Speak to the established agency owner throughout, never the beginner.
