**Output:** Full structured breakdown ready for YouTube script and Miro board — personas, awareness level thoughts, angles, formats, what works, Growthub reveal

## What This Skill Does

Produces the full analytical and scripting foundation for a Growthub YouTube brand breakdown video. The breakdown has two jobs: show the viewer something genuinely useful about how a scaled brand's creative strategy works, and demonstrate Growthub's process in a way that builds trust without a hard sell.

The output feeds directly into a YouTube script and Miro board brief.

---

## Inputs

**Step 1 — Brand name only (user provides)**
Claude researches the brand first: what they do, hero product, revenue/scale indicators, ad library volume, funnel structure. Output this before asking for ad transcripts.

**Step 2 — Ad transcripts (user provides)**
- Top 3 highest impression ads (copy + format description)
- Top 6 longest running ads (copy + format description)

Do not proceed to analysis until both steps are complete.

---

## Research Phase (Step 1)

When given the brand name, fetch and compile:

1. **Brand homepage** — hero product, core claim, positioning
2. **Product page** — mechanism, ingredients/features, offer structure (bundles, subscriptions, pricing)
3. **Meta Ad Library** — active ad count, historical ad count, media mix if visible, longest running creative
4. **Third-party review sites** (Trustpilot, Cult Beauty, Amazon if applicable) — what real customers say, recurring language, complaints
5. **Revenue/scale indicators** — public press, DTC databases, funding rounds, any verified figures. If no verifiable number exists, say so explicitly. Never estimate or guess revenue.

Output a Brand Overview section covering:
- What they sell and who they sell it to
- Hero product and offer structure
- Ad library scale (active ads, total historical)
- Any notable funnel elements (quiz funnels, advertorials, bundle mechanics)
- Verified revenue or scale indicator — if unverifiable, lead with ad count instead

### Why They're Scaling — Market Position Read

As part of the Brand Overview, include a short section answering: why is this brand growing?

This is not a summary of their marketing. It is a read on the structural reason they are winning. Two possible frames:

**Saturated market:** If they're operating in a crowded category (supplements, skincare, hair care, pet products), the question is: what is their differentiation? Is it product mechanism, offer structure, positioning, target persona, or creative format? Where did the category zig and they zagged? Name the specific decision that gives them an edge over the other brands in the feed.

**Innovating market:** If they're early in a category or creating demand rather than capturing it, the question is: what is the new behaviour or belief they're selling? What had to be true in culture for this brand to work now that wasn't true two years ago?

In either case, the goal is one clear sentence: the structural reason this brand is growing that other brands haven't figured out or aren't executing as well. This is the frame the viewer needs before the creative analysis lands.

If the reason is primarily product (not marketing), say so. If it's primarily creative and distribution, say so. If it's both, explain how the two reinforce each other.

## Analysis Phase (Step 2)

Once ad transcripts are provided, run the following in order. Do not skip steps or reorder them.

---

### Section 1 — Persona Analysis

Identify the personas being targeted across all 9 ads combined.

For each persona ask:
- Who is this person specifically? Age, life context, relationship with the problem.
- Is this persona broad (anyone with this symptom) or specific (defined life situation, identity, context)?
- How many of the 9 ads are pointed at this persona?

Flag if personas are too broad for 2026. The standard has shifted: clear persona definition with specific context outperforms broad demographic targeting. If their personas are vague, say so and name what a more specific version would look like.

Select the 3 primary personas. All 3 must be for the hero product/funnel — not for different products in their range. If they are targeting different products with different personas, note this, then focus analysis on the persona tied to the best-selling product.

Flag any personas that are absent from their creative but could work well for the hero product — especially if competitors are running creative to those audiences.

---

### Section 2 — Awareness Level Thoughts Per Persona

For each of the 3 personas, map the customer's internal monologue at each awareness level.

These are not descriptions of what Goli is doing at each level. These are what the customer is thinking before they encounter an ad.

Format for each persona:

**Persona name**
- Unaware: "[internal thought]"
- Problem Aware: "[internal thought]"
- Solution Aware: "[internal thought]"
- Product Aware: "[internal thought]"
- Most Aware: "[internal thought]"

Then note which awareness levels the brand's current ads are actually targeting. Flag over-concentration (e.g. 70% of ads at Most Aware) and what it means for their scaling ceiling.

---

### Section 3 — Winning Angles

Identify the top 3 angles being run across the 9 ads.

For each angle:
- Name it in one line
- Which ads demonstrate it (by number or description)
- Why it works — the psychological mechanism, not just the description
- Any executional observation (is it being run well, is the structure the same across every ad, is it getting repetitive)

Then identify missing angles — angles that would work for the hero product and persona set but are absent from their creative. Ground this in the awareness level map: if Problem Aware is underserved, what angle would reach someone at that level?

---

### Section 4 — Formats

Break down the format mix across the 9 ads:
- List each format present (skit, UGC testimonial, discount static, demonstration, founder story, etc.)
- Approximate % of the ad library each format represents based on what's visible
- For each format: one observation about why it's working or not working

**Missing formats — two sources:**

First, flag any format absent from this brand's library that would suit the persona and awareness level gaps identified in Sections 1-3.

Second, identify what similar fast-scaling brands in the same category are currently running that this brand isn't. This is the competitive format gap — not hypothetical, but observed. If brands at the same scale or higher are running a format that's absent here, name it, say which type of brand is running it, and explain why it's gaining traction. This gives the viewer something they can act on immediately.

**Creative Diversity Check (Andromeda/Entity ID)**

After mapping the formats, assess creative diversity risk.

Meta's Andromeda update groups structurally similar ads under the same entity ID and shows them to the same audience pool. If a brand's library is 70%+ one format or one concept structure, their ads are competing with each other for reach instead of expanding it.

Ask: if you stripped the hooks and looked only at structure — same narrator style, same scene setup, same edit rhythm — how many of these ads are effectively the same creative to Meta's algorithm?

Flag the risk level:
- **Low risk:** clear format diversity across the library, different structures reaching different audience buckets
- **Medium risk:** 1-2 dominant formats with minimal structural variation, reach starting to compress
- **High risk:** the majority of active ads are structurally identical, frequency rising, new launches cannibalising existing spend

Note this explicitly in the section. It sets up the Growthub Reveal.

---

### Section 5 — What Works (Viewer Takeaway)

This section is written for the YouTube viewer. It is the most shareable, most educational part of the video.

Identify the 1-2 things this brand is doing at a creative strategy level that actually works — not just "they have good UGC" but the specific structural or strategic decision that explains their performance.

For each, write:
- The observation (one clear sentence)
- The mechanism (why this works, at the psychological or algorithmic level)
- The takeaway (what a viewer could apply to their own brand)

This section should make the viewer feel they learned something they couldn't have figured out just by watching the ads themselves. That is the standard.

---

### Section 6 — The Growthub Reveal

This is the closing section of the video. It is not a hard sell. It is a demonstration.

Structure:

**What they currently have — the CS Map snapshot**
Before saying what to change, map what this brand actually has: which personas they're targeting, which awareness levels they're hitting, which angles they're running, and which formats they're using. This is the CS Map as it exists today. One short paragraph is enough — the analysis in Sections 1-5 has already done the work, this is the synthesis.

**What we'd preserve**
Pick 1-2 things from their creative strategy worth keeping. Explain why. This shows the viewer that Growthub's approach is analytical, not reflexively "we'd do it differently."

**What we'd change**
Pick 2-3 specific creative changes Growthub would make. These must connect directly back to the gaps identified in Sections 1-4: underserved personas, missing awareness levels, absent angles, format gaps, or creative diversity risk.

Write each change as a specific brief, not a general recommendation:
- Who the ad would target (persona + awareness level)
- What the angle would be
- What format it would use
- Why this would outperform what they're currently running

Where the creative diversity risk is medium or high, frame at least one recommended change explicitly as a new entity ID — a structurally different creative that Meta can distribute to a fresh audience pool rather than compete with existing spend. Name the format difference clearly: different narrator style, different scene structure, different edit rhythm. The goal is reach expansion, not just angle variation.

**What's working for similar brands right now**
Before closing, name 1-2 creative moves that are working for similar brands in the same category that are currently scaling fast. These are not hypotheticals — they are observed patterns from brands running comparable products to comparable audiences. Explain why these moves are gaining traction now. If Growthub is running something similar for a client in this space, reference the approach (not the client) and explain why it's outperforming more conventional creative.

This positions the recommendations as grounded in live market intelligence, not just gap analysis.

**The process point**
One paragraph explaining how Growthub approaches this for every client — the creative strategy map, the persona-to-awareness-level-to-angle framework, the testing architecture. Written in Lorenzo's voice. Not a pitch. A window into how the work gets done.

This section should end with the viewer thinking: "Growthub has a process that other agencies don't. I want that process running on my account." That is the only goal. Do not write a CTA inside the skill — the video CTA is handled separately.

## Output Order

Produce in this exact order:

1. Brand Overview (from research) — including Why They're Scaling
2. Persona Analysis
3. Awareness Level Thoughts Per Persona
4. Winning Angles
5. Formats — including competitor format benchmarks
6. What Works
7. Growthub Reveal — CS Map snapshot, what to preserve, what to change, what similar brands are doing

---

## Rules

- Never guess revenue. If no verifiable public figure exists, lead with ad volume as the scale indicator.
- All 3 personas must be for the same product/funnel.
- Awareness level thoughts are the customer's internal monologue — not a description of what the brand is doing.
- The "What Works" section must teach something that isn't obvious from watching the ads.
- The Growthub Reveal must connect directly to the gaps found in the analysis. Never introduce new observations in Section 6 that weren't grounded in Sections 1-4.
- Never write a hard sell in Section 6. The demonstration is the pitch.
