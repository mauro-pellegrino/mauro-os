1. Positioning Before Creatives

Skincare is one of the hardest niches to scale on Meta in 2026. CPMs are high, every ad in the feed looks the same, and you're competing against brands raising VC money with $200 CPAs.

Most brands try to copy the leader. That works when the leader started scaling within the last year. In a 5+ year saturated market, you need a different move.

New avatar
Take a category dominated by one demographic and rebuild it for another. Postpartum, perimenopausal, men's anti-aging — pick the audience the leader is ignoring.
Niche mechanism
Anchor on a new mechanism story for a tighter audience. "Postpartum skin barrier collapse." TAM stays at 1-5M people. Big enough to scale, narrow enough to dominate.
Layered play
New avatar plus new mechanism. The strongest play in saturated markets. "This product works specifically for [avatar] because of [new mechanism]."

You can't AB test positioning the way you AB test ads. Commit first, then test angles, formats, and funnels underneath it.

2. The Creative Strategy Map

Treat each buyer persona like its own business. 3 personas, 5 awareness stages each, 5-10 concepts per week per persona.

Andromeda compresses structurally similar ads into one audience pool. Scale now comes from 30 small winners stacking, not from finding one mega-ad.

5 stages
Unaware, Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware. Different script at each stage.
3-5 angles
Per persona, per awareness stage. Each angle enters from a different pain point.
3-5 formats
Per angle. Structurally distinct formats reach different audience pools.

Most skincare brands I audit run 50+ ads to one persona, at one awareness stage, in one format. Andromeda treats those as one ad with 50 hooks.

3. Format Spread (Kill the 2021 UGC)

The "I'm obsessed with this serum, let me show you why" UGC format is dead for cold traffic. Viewers scroll past before the third second. You need formats that don't read as ads in the first frame.

What's Burned Out
What's Working in 2026
Single-creator UGC excitement videos
Dermatologist or aesthetician authority talks
"Look at my skin" before/after with no mechanism
Podcast-style mechanism explainers (host + expert)
Ingredient lists and lab promises
Educational walks-and-talks (in-bathroom, in-store)

Same mechanism in 4 structurally different formats produces 4 different audience pools. Andromeda gives them different entity IDs because the structural footprint is different.

4. The Trust Mechanic

Skincare buyers are skeptical because they've been burned. Wrong ingredients, breakouts, $80 serums that did nothing. Your ad has to address the trust gap before it asks for the click.

Where Most Brands Go Wrong
The Trust Mechanic
Ingredient stack as the hero
Skin transformation as the hero, ingredients as proof
Generic before/after photos
Authority-narrated before/afters with timeline
No callout to the "I've tried everything" objection
Direct callout to past failures inside the ad copy

5. Subscription + 90-Day LTV

This is the lever that lets you outbid every competitor on Meta. Two numbers we track for every skincare client past $500K/month:

Sub opt-in
Target 80%+. Top brands hit 95%+. Volume discounts route customers to the 60-day or 90-day bundle by default.
90-day LTV
Skincare results take 60-90 days. Frame the timeline explicitly in funnel + email flows. Money-back guarantee at 90 days.
Outbid math
When your 90-day LTV is $180 and your competitor's is $90, you can outbid them on every cold-traffic auction. The CPA gap is the moat.

Brands that scale past $1M/month have a sharper map than brands that plateau. The volume of ads is downstream of the map quality.
