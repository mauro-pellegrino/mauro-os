## What This Subtype Is

A long-form post built around **a single video clip** plus a short caption. The video does the work. The caption frames it, anchors the credibility, and points the reader's attention.

Subtype identity: video-first. The caption is a hook for the video, not the deliverable itself. Different from `infographic-html-long-form.md` (which is image-driven) and `doc-screenshot-long-form.md` (which is single-image work-product driven).

The video can be:
- A screen recording (workflow walkthrough, system demo, dashboard scroll, before/after software change)
- A talking-head (Mauro to camera)
- An AI-generated demo asset (the proof-of-claim for an AI workflow)
- A montage of multiple clips edited into one (less common, higher production lift)

For Mauro's lane, screen recordings of the actual engine running are the highest-signal asset: they prove the system exists without requiring a camera.

## Pattern Variants (Inside This Format)

The video format hosts multiple content patterns. Pick one per post.

### Variant A: BOF Transformation Flex (time-compression)
- **Anchor:** a recent win where the speed-to-result is the differentiator
- **Caption hook:** "[N days]. [X invested]. [Y outcome]." (real numbers in first line, or bracketed placeholders)
- **Setup:** "The last version of this took [longer period]. This one did it in under [shorter period]."
- **Closer:** branded effect [CALIBRATE: Mauro's branded effect name not defined yet]
- **Video media:** the actual output (real work, anonymized if needed)
- **CTA:** No hard CTA (confidence positioning)

### Variant B: BOF Transformation Flex (named arc)
- **Anchor:** a named transformation arc (client or collaborator, with consent)
- **Caption hook:** "[First name] started when [low starting point]. Today [high outcome]."
- **Video media:** work from that engagement OR a clip of the person speaking (if they consent to being on camera)
- **CTA:** No hard CTA
- **Status:** activates when Mauro has named results. Until then use [CLIENT RESULT — fill from social-proof/ when available].

### Variant C: Result Showcase (numbers + reveal)
- **Anchor:** a striking result that can be reverse-engineered (external and public, or Mauro's own)
- **Caption hook:** "[X] got [N] [outcome]. None of it is manual. Every step is AI."
- **Setup:** stack reveal ("[Tool A] for X. [Tool B] for Y.") + production reality ("No writers, no editors, no agency retainer.")
- **Video media:** the AI-generated or workflow-produced asset itself
- **CTA:** Soft consultative OR no CTA
- **Important:** no autodm CTA on this variant (that's lead-magnet territory). Keep the reveal + reality, drop any "Comment X" payoff.

### Variant D: Tactical Walkthrough (screen recording)
- **Anchor:** a workflow worth demoing
- **Caption hook:** problem-bottleneck opener ("One of the biggest bottlenecks I kept running into was [X]")
- **Setup:** minimal-stack reveal ("Two tools. Nothing else required.")
- **Video media:** screen recording of the workflow in motion, sped up if longer than 30 sec
- **CTA:** Soft consultative OR tier-gated DM trigger if the workflow warrants qualifier filtering

### Variant E: Operator Stance (talking head)
- **Anchor:** a position Mauro wants to plant publicly (pull candidates from the 15 beliefs in `brand/business-context-answers.md`)
- **Caption hook:** pattern-interrupt opener ("You're probably expecting me to say [obvious answer]. But the reality is [counter-claim]")
- **Video media:** Mauro talking to camera, 15-30 sec
- **CTA:** Soft consultative

### Variant F: Result + Replicable Layer (workflow video)
- **Anchor:** the workflow itself is novel enough to be the artifact
- **Caption hook:** "[Result] from this [workflow]. Walk you through how to copy it STEP-BY-STEP."
- **Video media:** screen recording of the workflow + result image in caption thread or attached
- **CTA:** Thread funnel (step-by-step in the thread underneath)

### Variant G: AI Tutorial Short (external pattern)
- **Anchor:** AI workflow that produces a visible output, taught in 2-5 mins
- **Caption hook:** lowercase capability claim OR "here's how to do this with AI in [time]:"
  - "ai [workflow] is fire. it cost me less than $[X]."
  - "here's how to do this with ai in 2 mins:"
  - "here's how to clone any [asset] in 60 seconds:"
- **Body:** 3-5 dash bullets, lowercase, each step under 10 words
- **Closer (signature move):** dollar / time savings line
  - "congrats you just saved $[X] and [N] weeks of waiting."
- **Video media:** AI-generated demo video (5-60 sec, the proof-of-claim asset)
- **Distribution mechanic:** often a quote-tweet of someone asking "how do they do this?" — built-in audience tap
- **CTA:** No CTA. The video IS the close.
- **Voice:** lowercase peer-to-peer throughout. [CALIBRATE: this register differs from Mauro's default full-sentence voice. Confirm with him before shipping it on @maurojpelle; savings claims must be real or bracketed.]
- **Caption length:** 30-80 words total (tight)
- **Engagement reality (observed on the studied external account):** consistently the highest-performing shape in the studied set.

### Variant H: Provocative Challenge + AI Asset (external pattern)
- **Anchor:** an AI-produced asset worth showcasing
- **Caption hook:** confrontational question or stance
  - "Stop making boring AI content."
  - "If your 'content team' isn't producing this, what are they doing?"
- **Body:** 1-3 short lines on why this matters or what's missing in the industry
- **Video media:** the AI-generated asset
- **CTA:** Soft consultative OR none. Sometimes followed up with a separate quote-tweet promoting a community / playbook.
- **Caption length:** 30-60 words
- **Engagement reality (observed):** lower reach than Variant G but high comment ratio when the provocation lands.

### Variant I: Work Showcase (system output + observation)
- **Anchor:** an actual asset the engine just shipped, used as a showcase of what the system produces
- **Caption hook:** brief observation about what's working, e.g. "[Format] is crushing it for [audience] right now." or "[Recognizable company] is doing [X]. What's stopping you?"
- **Body:** 1-2 lines on why the format works
- **Video media:** the actual output (anonymized client context, real production)
- **CTA:** Soft consultative ("Link in my bio.")
- **Caption length:** 30-90 words
- **Engagement reality:** lower views than TOF education variants. BOF showcase positioning by design; grade by inbound DM quality, not views.

## Caption Format Rotation (grounded in the account's own analytics)

Variant I (work showcase) is not a single caption shape. Several recur, and posting the same one every time flattens the feed. Before drafting a video long-form, **rotate the caption format and ground the choice in what @maurojpelle's own X analytics show is working.** Do not default to the "here's why this works" bullets every time.

[CALIBRATE: the rotation table below lists the recurring shapes; the performance signals need to be rebuilt from Mauro's own analytics exports once the account has volume.]

| Format | Caption shape |
|---|---|
| Methodology bullets | "Another output from the engine. Here's why it works: [3-4 bullets]" |
| Ultra-short curiosity | Near-empty caption, the video carries it. "Run a [niche] agency? Study this." |
| Prescriptive niche + example | Lead with a teachable principle, video is the proof. "Here's what every [niche] agency needs:" |
| Capability / setup flex | "When your content system runs itself..." what the setup unlocks |
| Personal reaction / BTS | Founder voice. "Really happy how this came out..." |
| One-lesson truth | Showcase + one sharp lesson. "[Showcase]... but here's the truth: [lesson]" |
| Hybrid (curiosity + bullets) | Ultra-short curiosity hook, then 3-4 methodology bullets |

**SOP — adapt the format from analytics each time:**
1. Pull the latest per-post X content export if available.
2. Rank video/showcase posts by impressions, note the caption format each used.
3. Pick a format the account has NOT run in its last few showcase posts. Rotate, don't repeat.
4. Match the format to the topic signal in the data.
5. Tag the post with the format used so the content calendar can track variety.

Caption length still follows the Variant I target (30-90 words on X). The hybrid runs slightly longer because of the bullets. When no fresh analytics export is on hand, rotate by what the recent posts used, never repeat the last showcase's format.

## Media Spec (Video Production)

### Format
- 9:16 vertical (X feed performance > 16:9 horizontal for in-feed engagement)
- Under 30 sec for autoplay loop
- 1080p minimum
- No watermarks, no branded intros (the video is the work, not the marketing)

### Anonymization rules
- Client logos: blur or replace with generic placeholder if the client isn't being named publicly
- Client URLs / identifiable assets: blur if recoverable to identity
- Faces / voices: only show with a release or consent
- Dashboard / analytics footage: blur any client-identifying selector, keep numbers + date range visible

### Production reality
- Most videos already exist (screen recordings of the engine, shipped assets)
- Mauro pulls the file, drops it into the post
- For tactical walkthroughs (Variant D): screen recording via macOS QuickTime or Loom export, edited in CapCut
- For talking-head (Variant E): self-recorded on phone, edited in CapCut for captions and pacing

### Markup in skill output
When the skill produces a draft, mark the video insertion point as:
`[MEDIA: short description of the video — variant + duration + visual focus]`

Example:
`[MEDIA: 28-sec screen recording, 9:16 vertical, the weekly content loop running end to end, client names blurred]`

## Anti-Patterns (Specific to This Subtype)

- Caption longer than the video can hold attention. The video is the deliverable. Captions over 200 words on X bury the play button.
- Video without anonymization when the win's rules say the client should be anonymized. Cross-check before publishing.
- Autodm CTA on a long-form video post. If you want autodm, this is a lead magnet — use `skills/lead-gen/lead-magnet/youtube-video.md`. Don't mix.
- 16:9 horizontal video on X. Performs worse than 9:16 in-feed.
- Talking-head video over 45 sec without sharp cuts. Attention drops fast on X.
- Variant mismatch: time-compression caption with a non-time-compression video, or operator-stance caption with a screen-recording video. Caption + video have to be in the same variant family.
- Adding a CTA after a Variant A or B caption. Kills the confidence positioning.
