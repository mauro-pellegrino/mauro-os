## What This Subtype Is

A long-form post built around **a single video clip from the team** plus a short caption. The video does the work. The caption frames it, anchors the credibility, and points the reader's attention. Most common production format on Lorenzo's account.

Subtype identity: video-first. The caption is a hook for the video, not the deliverable itself. Different from `infographic-html-long-form.md` (which is image-driven) and `doc-screenshot-long-form.md` (which is single-image work-product driven).

The video can be:
- A real ad creative (spokesperson, podcast-style, street interview, documentary)
- A screen recording (workflow walkthrough, dashboard scroll, before/after software change)
- A talking-head (Lorenzo or team member to camera)
- A montage of multiple clips edited into one (less common, higher production lift)

## Pattern Variants (Inside This Format)

The video format hosts multiple content patterns. Pick one per post.

### Variant A: BOF Transformation Flex (time-compression)
- **Anchor:** a recent win where the speed-to-result is the differentiator
- **Caption hook:** "[N days]. $[X]+ spent. ~[Y] [outcome]." (numbers in first line)
- **Setup:** "Our last batch of winning ads took [longer period]. This one did it in under [shorter period]."
- **Closer:** branded effect ("Stealth Creatives shipped by the team")
- **Video media:** the actual ad creative (real client work, anonymized if needed)
- **CTA:** No hard CTA (confidence positioning)
- **Source example:** `accounts/lorenzo-x/drafts/post-006-spokesperson-11-days.md` (Win 001)

### Variant B: BOF Transformation Flex (named-client arc)
- **Anchor:** a named client transformation arc
- **Caption hook:** "[Client first name] hired us when [low starting point]. Today [high outcome]."
- **Video media:** ad creative from that client OR a clip of the client speaking (if they consent to being on camera)
- **CTA:** No hard CTA
- **Source example:** post-004 Variant A from `research/post-studies/`

### Variant C: Result Showcase (numbers + reveal)
- **Anchor:** a striking external result the team can reverse-engineer
- **Caption hook:** "[X] got [N] [outcome]. None of it is real. Every frame is AI."
- **Setup:** stack reveal ("[Tool A] for X. [Tool B] for Y.") + production reality ("No actors, no shoots, no editing team.")
- **Video media:** the AI-generated or workflow-produced asset itself
- **CTA:** Soft consultative OR no CTA depending on account voice
- **Source pattern:** Mike Futia post 003 ("AI UGC is f*cking cracked") — adapted to Growthub voice
- **Important:** When porting from Mike Futia's style, strip the autodm CTA (lead-magnet territory). For long-form, keep the reveal + reality, drop the "Comment X" payoff.

### Variant D: Tactical Walkthrough (screen recording)
- **Anchor:** a workflow worth demoing
- **Caption hook:** problem-bottleneck opener ("One of the biggest bottlenecks we kept running into was [X]")
- **Setup:** minimal-stack reveal ("Two tools. Nothing else required.")
- **Video media:** screen recording of the workflow in motion, sped up if longer than 30 sec
- **CTA:** Soft consultative OR tier-gated DM trigger if the workflow warrants spend-qualifier filtering
- **Source pattern:** post-010 article structure compressed into video + caption

### Variant E: Operator Stance (talking head)
- **Anchor:** a position the operator wants to plant publicly
- **Caption hook:** pattern-interrupt opener ("You're probably expecting me to say [obvious answer]. But the reality is [counter-claim]")
- **Video media:** Lorenzo (or team member) talking to camera, 15-30 sec
- **CTA:** Soft consultative
- **Source pattern:** compressed post-009 manifesto delivered as on-camera statement

### Variant F: Result + Replicable Layer (workflow video)
- **Anchor:** the workflow itself is novel enough to be the artifact
- **Caption hook:** "[Result] from this [workflow]. Walk you through how to copy it STEP-BY-STEP."
- **Video media:** screen recording of the workflow + result image in caption thread or attached
- **CTA:** Thread funnel (step-by-step in the thread underneath)

### Variant G: AI Tutorial Short (0xROAS template)
- **Anchor:** AI workflow that produces a video output, taught in 2-5 mins
- **Caption hook:** lowercase capability claim OR "here's how to do this with AI in [time]:"
  - "ai ugc yapping is fire. it cost me less than $0.50."
  - "here's how to do this with ai in 2 mins:"
  - "ai ugc is literally crazy."
  - "here's how to clone any video in 60 seconds:"
- **Body:** 3-5 dash bullets, lowercase, each step under 10 words
- **Closer (signature move):** dollar / time savings line
  - "congrats you just saved $10k and 3 weeks of waiting on an animation."
  - "congrats you just saved $200 and 2 days of work."
- **Video media:** AI-generated demo video (5-60 sec, the proof-of-claim asset)
- **Distribution mechanic:** often a quote-tweet of someone asking "how do they do this?" — built-in audience tap
- **CTA:** No CTA. The video IS the close.
- **Voice:** lowercase peer-to-peer throughout. Profanity allowed in measured doses ("$0.50" not "$0.5", "fucking" allowed on @stealth_ai voice but NOT Lorenzo's voice per voice.md).
- **Caption length:** 30-80 words total (tight)
- **Source examples (10 captured 2026-05-27):** `research/external-accounts/0xROAS/` — Nov 6 2025 through May 25 2026
- **Engagement reality:** consistently 24K-159K views per post. Highest-performing variant in the captured dataset.

### Variant H: Provocative Challenge + AI Ad (Camilo Castañeda template)
- **Anchor:** an AI ad creative worth showcasing
- **Caption hook:** confrontational question or stance
  - "Stop making boring AI ads."
  - "WHOS MAKING BETTER CLAYMATION RIGHT NOW?"
  - "If your 'creative team' isn't making ads like this, are they even creative?"
- **Body:** 1-3 short lines on why this matters or what's missing in the industry
- **Video media:** AI-generated ad creative (claymation, podcast-style, character-driven)
- **CTA:** Soft consultative OR none. Sometimes followed up with a separate quote-tweet promoting a community / playbook (Skool, etc.)
- **Caption length:** 30-60 words total
- **Source examples (4 captured 2026-05-27):** `research/external-accounts/camilocastaneda/` — May 19 to May 22 2026
- **Engagement reality:** 3K-45K views per post. Lower than 0xROAS but high comment ratio when the provocation lands.

### Variant I: Team Showcase + Ad Creative (Lorenzo's pattern)
- **Anchor:** an actual ad creative the team just shipped, used as a showcase of what the agency produces
- **Caption hook:** brief observation about what's working
  - "Another day, another banger from the team. PIXAR ADS are crushing it right now."
  - "A brand BOUGHT by unilever is testing pixar ads. What's stopping you from doing the same?"
- **Body:** 1-2 lines on why the format works ("Our formats become top spenders because we have the perfect editing & scriptwriting that makes our ads look like CONTENT.")
- **Video media:** the actual ad creative (anonymized client, real production)
- **CTA:** Soft consultative ("Link in my bio." OR "Want more creative diversity for your brand? Link in my bio.")
- **Caption length:** 30-90 words total
- **Source examples (2 captured 2026-05-27):** `accounts/lorenzo-x/shipped/` — May 21 and May 25 2026
- **Engagement reality:** 7.5K-9.9K views. Lower than TOF education variants. BOF showcase positioning by design — grade by inbound DM quality, not views.

## Caption Format Rotation (grounded in X analytics)

Variant I (team showcase) is not a single caption shape. The account runs several, and posting the same one every time flattens the feed. Before drafting a video long-form, **rotate the caption format and ground the choice in what the account's own X analytics show is working that week.** Do not default to the "here's why these work" bullets every time.

**The recurring video-LF caption formats (observed in Lorenzo + Bogdan analytics, W25 / June 2026):**

| Format | Caption shape | W25 signal |
|---|---|---|
| Methodology bullets | "Another ad from the team. Here's why these work: [3-4 bullets]" | Lorenzo authority post (4,878) |
| Ultra-short curiosity | Near-empty caption, the video carries it. "Own a [niche] brand? Study this." | Bogdan "Own a pet brand? Learn from this" (12,934) |
| Prescriptive niche + example | Lead with a teachable principle, video is the proof. "Here's what every [niche] brand needs:" | Lorenzo "every pet brand needs…" (7,214) |
| Capability / studio flex | "When you have a studio & actors…" what the setup unlocks | Lorenzo (4,081), Bogdan "Team is cooking…" (4,582) |
| Personal reaction / BTS | Founder voice. "Really happy how this came out for a [product]…" | Lorenzo seat-cushion post (2,928) |
| One-lesson truth | Showcase + one sharp lesson. "Another banger… but here's the truth: [lesson]" | Bogdan (1,307) |
| Hybrid (curiosity + bullets) | Ultra-short curiosity hook, then 3-4 methodology bullets | Confirmed by Mauro 2026-06-24 (pet authority post) |

**SOP — adapt the format from analytics each time:**
1. Pull the latest per-post X content export (both profiles) if available.
2. Rank video/showcase posts by impressions, note the caption format each used.
3. Pick a format the account has NOT run in its last few showcase posts. Rotate, don't repeat.
4. Match the format to the niche signal. Example: pet posts perform best as ultra-short curiosity or prescriptive-niche, not bullets.
5. Tag the post with the format used so the content calendar can track variety (ties into the calendar's format/topic tagging).

Caption length still follows the Variant I target (30-90 words on X). The hybrid runs slightly longer because of the bullets. When no fresh analytics export is on hand, rotate by what the recent posts in `accounts/lorenzo-x/` used, never repeat the last showcase's format.

## Media Spec (Video Production)

### Format
- 9:16 vertical (X feed performance > 16:9 horizontal for in-feed engagement)
- Under 30 sec for autoplay loop
- 1080p minimum
- No watermarks, no Growthub-branded intros (video is the work, not the marketing)

### Anonymization rules
- Client logos: blur or replace with generic placeholder if client isn't being named publicly
- Client URLs / product photography: blur if recoverable to identity
- Spokesperson faces / voices: only show if model release exists
- Dashboard / Ads Manager footage: blur client name selector, keep numbers + date range visible

### Production reality
- Most videos already exist (team-shot ad creatives, screen recordings from operators)
- Mauro pulls the file, drops it into the post
- For tactical walkthroughs (Variant D): screen recording via macOS QuickTime or Loom export, edited in CapCut
- For talking-head (Variant E): self-recorded on phone, edited in CapCut for captions and pacing

### Markup in skill output
When the skill produces a draft, mark the video insertion point as:
`[MEDIA: short description of the video — variant + duration + visual focus]`

Example:
`[MEDIA: 28-sec spokesperson ad video, 9:16 vertical, woman holding product, anonymized brand name on packaging]`

## Anti-Patterns (Specific to This Subtype)

- Caption longer than the video can hold attention. The video is the deliverable. Captions over 200 words on X bury the play button.
- Video without anonymization when wins-log says client should be anonymized. Cross-check the wins-log entry before publishing.
- Mike-Futia-style autodm CTA on a long-form video post. If you want autodm, this is a lead magnet — use `skills/lead-gen/lead-magnet/youtube-video.md`. Don't mix.
- 16:9 horizontal video on X. Performs worse than 9:16 in-feed.
- Talking-head video over 45 sec without sharp cuts. Attention drops fast on X.
- Variant mismatch: time-compression caption with a non-time-compression video, or operator-stance caption with a screen-recording video. Caption + video have to be in the same variant family.
- Adding a CTA after a Variant A or B caption. Kills the confidence positioning.
