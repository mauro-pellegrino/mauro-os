**Rule 1: No em dashes, ever.**
Em dashes (—) are completely banned in all Growthub content. Replace with a comma, period, or rewrite.

**Why:** Explicitly in the voice guide as a hard ban. Mauro has corrected this multiple times and is frustrated by it recurring.

**How to apply:** Before outputting any article, script, or copy, do a final check for em dashes. Zero tolerance.

---

**Rule 2: No "It's not X, it's Y" constructions and all variants.**
Includes: "These aren't templates. They're structures." / "It's not about volume. It's about variety." / "You're not guessing. You're testing." — any sentence that sets up a false contrast to sound profound.

**Why:** Dead giveaway AI writing pattern. Mauro caught it in the hooks script and was frustrated it appeared despite being in the voice guide.

**How to apply:** Just say the thing directly. "Variety matters more than volume." Not "It's not about volume, it's about variety."

---

**Rule 3: No trailing summaries after completing work.**
Don't summarize what you just did at the end of a response. Mauro can read the output.

**Why:** Wastes space and time. Mauro prefers terse responses.

**How to apply:** After completing a task, end the response. Don't recap the changes you made unless specifically asked.

---

**Rule 5: Autodm post copy — opening must be a bold one-liner provocation.**
Never open with a narrative setup or context paragraph. The first line must be punchy, direct, and provocative. Bullets are short fragments with CAPS on key metrics/words. X CTA = "Like + Comment [KEYWORD]". LinkedIn CTA = "Want me to send it over?" + numbered steps. DM = link only, soft upsell, reply-based CTA — no A/B question, no booking link.

**Why:** Mauro corrected the first autodm draft across all three components. The opening was a story setup instead of a one-liner, bullets were explanatory sentences, and the DM was over-engineered with an A/B question.

**How to apply:** Every time yt-to-autodm skill is used, enforce these patterns before outputting anything.

---

**Rule 4: No 3-item short parallel sentence stacks.**
Never write three short parallel items as separate lines or sentences. "Money-back guarantee. Free trial. Low-commitment entry." is the pattern to kill. Applies to any triplet of short punchy items used for rhythm or emphasis.

**Why:** Mauro explicitly flagged this as a hated pattern. It sounds like copywriting filler, not real writing.

**How to apply:** Merge into one sentence or rewrite as prose. "They offer a 90-day guarantee with a free trial entry point" beats three stacked lines every time.
