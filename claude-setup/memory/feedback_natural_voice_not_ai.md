For normal posts (not auto-DMs), write the way a real person actually types, not the AI-punchy style of super-short "trying to be interesting" sentences. Lorenzo's read: the punchy style "looks AI, people know it's fake." Benchmark = his own SOP / PoeticHQ quote-tweet ("hired a media buyer. sharp kid. spent his whole first month learning our launch process... thought a better SOP would make him more efficient. so I wrote one (38 pages). he hasn't even opened it."). Real story, normal sentences, a small honest admission, lands on a natural insight.

**Why:** Accounts that perform (he named Alex Cooper-type) aren't creative-to-stop-people. They just look real and natural, like something a person wrote. Lorenzo only cares what drives results, not his opinion.

**How to apply:** Story-led, conversational, full sentences over staccato fragments. Stop forcing numbers-led punchy hooks on opinion/education posts. EXCEPTION: auto-DM posts still use the punchy format (Lorenzo confirmed those have to be that way). This rule is for normal posts only. Casing is settled: standard capitalization (sentence case) is the house style, confirmed against the account analytics (~91% of letter-opening posts are capitalized). Natural voice does NOT mean lowercase. ALL-CAPS only for the occasional emphasis word. Full spec: `accounts/lorenzo-x/post-voice-positioning.md`. See also [[feedback_expert_not_beginner_positioning]] and [[feedback_writing_style]].
