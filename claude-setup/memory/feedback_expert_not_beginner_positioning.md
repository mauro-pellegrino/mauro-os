Write to the ideal buyer: the established agency owner who already runs a real business at scale. Never a beginner. Posts framed as "should you / shouldn't you use AI" read like we're talking to someone still on the outside of the game.

Reframe beginner questions into expert stances. Instead of "should you use AI in your content," say something like "surprised how many agency owners are still debating whether to use AI for content. the ones winning inbound settled that a while ago. the real question is how you wire it into your actual work so the output sounds like you."

**Why:** The ICP is established agency owners. Talking down to them as beginners loses the people we actually want in the DMs. A defined positioning (who we speak to, how sophisticated, how we sound) is high-leverage so posts stop being random.

**How to apply:** Default opinion/education hook = state what the winners already do as settled fact, then show how, rather than posing the beginner's yes/no question. The positioning/voice source of truth is `brand/positioning.md` + `brand/voice.md` (also feeds YouTube voice). See [[feedback_natural_voice_not_ai]], [[project_mauro_personal_brand]].
