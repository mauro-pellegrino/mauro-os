Write to the ideal buyer: the sophisticated brand owner (Lorenzo's persona) who already runs ads at scale. Not the expert media-buyer necessarily, but never a beginner. Lorenzo's flag: posts framed as "should you / shouldn't you use AI" read like we're talking to someone outside the direct-performance world.

Reframe beginner questions into expert stances. Instead of "should you use AI in your ad launch," say "I can't believe how many brands are still wondering whether to use AI. one of the biggest brands has 6000 active ads and they all use AI. you don't debate it, you use it the way the winners do: to maximize diversity and quantity."

**Why:** The ICP is brand owners like Lorenzo. Talking down to them as beginners loses the people we actually want in the DMs. He thinks a defined positioning (who we speak to, how sophisticated, how we sound) is likely high-leverage so posts stop being random.

**How to apply:** Default opinion/education hook = state what the winners already do as settled fact, then show how, rather than posing the beginner's yes/no question. Build a 1-page positioning/voice spec as source of truth (also feeds YouTube voice). See [[feedback_natural_voice_not_ai]], [[project_lorenzo_x_account]].
